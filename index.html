Noto Sans

Noto Sans is part of the typeface family called Noto, designed by Google to cover all languages and scripts of the world. The project began in 2012 with the idea of a harmonious design across the different writing systems. The Noto Sans font is sans-serif and was initially designed under the guidance of Monotype, inspired by earlier created typefaces Droid Sans and Open Sans. Soon, with its clean and modern appeal, Noto Sans had wide appeal and application both in digital and print media.

Among the major features included in Noto Sans are broad language coverage, catering to over 800 languages and several writing systems that range from Latin through Cyrillic and Greek to even obscure scripts. This makes them fit for multilingual projects facing the challenge of making texts in different languages appear harmonious. The font is clear and readable; strokes are even with open apertures, which allows distinguishing between characters. Noto Sans is very common in digital interfaces, educational materials, and websites, where readability through a wide range of screen sizes may be required.

The Noto project at Google was enormous, with years of research coordinated with linguists and font designers. In developing Noto Sans, the group tried to avoid any need for fallback fonts in order to render the text harmoniously and consistently across platforms. Licensed under the SIL Open Font License, Noto Sans is free to use and therefore can be used by anyone, from enterprises down to individuals working on multilingual or international communications projects.
